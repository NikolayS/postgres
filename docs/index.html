The Postgres Decade

a contribution review
2016 — 2025
source · robert m. haas, pgsql-hackers + git
10 years · 23,492 commits · ∼2.3M lines
compiled by NikolayS

Ten years of git log, one long sentence: more hands every year, more mail every year, more new faces every year — and yet a narrow band of committers still carries the keys. This is what a decade of PostgreSQL source-level contribution looks like when you lay it flat on paper.

№06

Pareto lives here

author concentration, 2025 · fig 6

Half the authors, contributing the smallest volumes, account for roughly one part in fifty of the year's patched lines. That is the long tail you will see plotted in §07 as a staircase — most of Postgres's 266 authors in 2025 shipped a single commit.

Postgres is a two-world project. People argue on pgsql-hackers; then, sometimes, patches land. Below, every person visible in either world in 2025, plotted on a log-log field: emails on one axis, lines of code authored on the other. Few names live in both extremes.

Source

Data tables contributions2025.dmp by Robert Haas (EDB), published at sites.google.com/site/robertmhaas/contributions. Context thread: pgsql-hackers.

Method

xlines = raw insertions from git log --shortstat -w -M, with mechanical commits (pgindent, translations, reverts) zeroed out. Authors read from the first Author: / Co-Authored-By: trailer. Names unified per the pgsql-hackers thread.
